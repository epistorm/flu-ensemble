Weighted Interval Score (WIS)

The Weighted Interval Score is a proper scoring rule for quantile forecasts. It generalizes the absolute error to probabilistic predictions by evaluating the sharpness and calibration of prediction intervals.

WIS (Raw) shows the mean Weighted Interval Score — lower is better.

The WIS ratio (WIS / Baseline) compares each model's mean WIS to the FluSight-baseline model's mean WIS over the same time period and horizons. Values below 1.0 indicate the model outperforms the baseline.

Coverage measures the fraction of observations that fall within a given prediction interval. Well-calibrated models should have coverage close to the nominal level (e.g., 50% PI should cover ~50% of observations).
